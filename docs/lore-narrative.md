## World Setting

**Year:** 2382  
**Location:** Planetary Core 9-C — The Obsidian Crust  
**Genre:** Industrial steampunk sci-fi

Planetary Core 9-C is a rogue geological body detected by deep space exploration arrays. It has no atmosphere, no stellar orbit, and no mercy. Compressed tectonic plates wrap a volatile radioactive interior — an environment prospectors call **The Obsidian Crust**.

Within hyper-pressurized fractures, friction and cosmic radiation fuse heavy metals into super-dense geode deposits enriched with **NMG (Neox Miner Guild) crystals** — sub-space particulate matter capable of near-instantaneous atomic fusing.

## Mining Zones

### Surface Crust
The outer shell of Core 9-C. Relatively stable gravitational stress. Rich in Copper, Iron, and Coal. Where every Guild Master begins their descent. The fractures glow faintly orange — a warning of what lies below.

### Mid-Fracture
Deeper corridors where pressure intensifies. Mithril veins appear alongside standard ores. Star-Element traces detected at 5% frequency. Fatigue accumulates faster. Only experienced operatives thrive here.

### Deep Core
Extreme gravitational stress zones near the radioactive interior. Maximum ore density — 30% Mithril, 10% Star-Element. Radiation levels require Cyborg-class miners. The guilds that control Deep Core operations control the ingot market.

---

## Miner Classes

### Biotech Miners
Organic-adapted extraction units engineered for moderate-depth operations. Lighter, cheaper, ideal for Surface and Mid-Fracture work. Common and Rare tiers available for Gold hire.

*"They breathe the crust so you don't have to."*

### Cyborg MINERs
Heavily shielded industrial units built for Deep Core deployment. Enhanced radiation resistance, superior extraction rates. Epic and Legendary tiers require NMG investment. Limited Legendary supply per epoch.

*"Forged for the depths. Paid for in fire."*

## Foundry Narrative

When ores reach the orbital foundry, the process is violent and precise. Raw material is injected into high-temperature fusion chambers. NMG catalyst — measured in fractions of a token — triggers near-instant atomic restructuring. What emerges is not ore. It is an ingot: proof that industrial labor created something the Interstellar Merchants Coalition will pay for in liquid NMG.

The foundry hums. The guild ledger updates. Another ingot enters the supply chain.

---

## Interstellar Merchants Coalition

Who buys the ingots? The **Interstellar Merchants Coalition** — a decentralized trade network operating beyond any single planetary jurisdiction. They purchase high-purity NMG-Ingots as industrial-grade catalyst stock, reselling to fusion plants, shipyards, and research stations across the outer systems.

They do not care how you mined the ore. They care that the ingot is pure and the NMG redemption is valid.

## Failure States

**Radiation damage:** Ores exposed too long in Deep Core zones degrade in quality. Dispatch recall before spoilage.

**Fatigue collapse:** Miners pushed beyond endurance stop producing. Camp rest or chemical cooling required.

**Durability failure:** Equipment worn to zero cannot deploy. Repair at orbital dock.
